# Advice Animals

## Overview
Advice Animals is a series of image macros featuring animals (sometimes humans) with captioned text representing a character trait or archetype. The images are typically placed on a colorful wheel background, though some use unedited versions of the original photo.

## Origin
The name "Advice Animals" is taken from the [Advice Dog](https://knowyourmeme.com/memes/advice-dog) image macro series that originated on September 7th, 2006. The image was first posted on the Mushroom Kingdom forums, and displayed a labrador puppy with the iconic color wheel background. The creator, Evan Herrington, said he was inspired by the "royal rainbow" from the Katamari Damacy video game series.

## Spread
The first web application to create this kind of image macro was built by Ferenc Somos and hosted on Memegenerator.net (registered March 18th, 2009). Other builders exist on Memebase, Quickmeme, and an iTunes application. The /r/AdviceAnimals subreddit was created on December 7, 2010 and gave rise to many derivatives including Misunderstood D-Bag, Rasta Science Teacher, Hood Dad, Internet Husband, Harmless Scout Leader, and Schrute Facts. As of August 2011, the subreddit had over 56,000 subscribers.

## Derivatives
Certain subcultures created their own Advice Animals variants:
- **Pokémon** → Pokeparents, Dat Ash, Professor Oak (Advice Oak)
- **Lolcats** → Business Cat, Lenin Cat
- **Rage Comics** → Forever Alone, Y U No Guy

These derivatives are used mainly within their respective fanbases, employing jokes understood by community members.

## Cultural Impact
Advice Animals became a ubiquitous meme format, influencing countless spin-offs and remaining a recognizable part of internet meme history.
