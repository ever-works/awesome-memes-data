# Dancing Baby

## Overview
The Dancing Baby is a 3D-rendered animation of a baby dancing the cha-cha, widely recognized as one of the first internet memes.

## Origin
Created in 1996 by Michael Girard, Robert Lurye, and John Chadwick using Character Studio software, the animation was originally a technical demo to showcase realistic movement.

## Spread
The file spread via email chains and early web forums in the late 1990s, gaining popularity on sites like Albino Blacksheep and later on television shows such as *Ally McBeal*. Its widespread sharing exemplified early viral content before the advent of social media.

## Cultural Impact
As one of the earliest examples of an internet meme, the Dancing Baby illustrates how digital media can be remixed, shared, and transformed by users, paving the way for modern meme culture.

## Notes
The animation is often cited in discussions about the evolution of memes and the concept of viral media.
