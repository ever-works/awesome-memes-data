## Overview

Success Kid is an Internet meme characterized by a photo of a baby (Sammy Griner) with a determined expression, clutching a fistful of sand. The meme is used to convey feelings of success or triumph over minor achievements.

## Origin

The original photograph was taken by Laney Griner (mother) and uploaded to Flickr on August 31, 2007, under the title "Why I oughta...". The photo shows her son, Sam, at the beach attempting to eat sand.

## Evolution

Initially, the image was used in memes with captions like "I Hate Sandcastles," implying the boy had destroyed another child's sandcastle. However, the interpretation shifted to focus on the boy's expression as a gesture of self-congratulation, leading to the "Success Kid" meme with captions celebrating small victories and good fortune.

## Commercial Use and Licensing

Laney Griner licensed the image through Getty Images and later managed licensing herself. The image has appeared in advertisements for Coca-Cola and Virgin Mobile UK, and was used by the White House in 2013 to promote immigration reform. Griner has also taken legal action against unauthorized use, including a lawsuit against a fireworks company and a cease-and-desist letter to a U.S. Representative.

## Notable Uses

- In 2013, the White House tweeted the Success Kid meme to support immigration reform legislation.
- In 2015, the Griner family used the meme in a GoFundMe campaign to raise funds for Sam's father's kidney transplant, which gained significant traction after being shared on Reddit.
- The meme has been featured on merchandise sold by Hot Topic.

## Cultural Impact

Success Kid has been described as "likely the Internet's most famous baby" by CNN. The meme's popularity has led to discussions about the ethics of monetizing a child's image, which the Griner family has addressed by emphasizing the joy the meme brings to people.
