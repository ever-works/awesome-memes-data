## Overview\n\nLoss is a four-panel comic strip from the webcomic Ctrl+Alt+Del published on June 2, 2008, depicting the miscarriage of the character Lilah. The strip's dramatic shift from comedy to tragedy led to widespread mockery and parody online.\n\n## Origin\n\nCreated by Tim Buckley, the strip titled \"Loss\" appeared in Ctrl+Alt+Del and was intended as a serious storyline. The unexpected tone shift provoked a negative reaction from readers, sparking anti-fandom and meme creation.\n\n## Spread\n\nFollowing its release, the strip was parodied by numerous webcomics and internet communities. On 4chan's /v/ board, users began creating minimalist representations using vertical bars and underscores, such as \"| || || |_\", leading to the phrase \"Is this Loss?\" as a meme format. The meme proliferated across platforms, inspiring countless variations and remixes.\n\n## Legacy\n\nOver a decade later, Loss remains one of the internet's most recognizable memes, referenced in articles by Polygon, io9, and Select All. Tim Buckley has acknowledged the meme's persistence, sometimes finding it amusing. The strip's ten-year anniversary in 2018 saw Buckley publish a self-referential parody on the comic's website.
