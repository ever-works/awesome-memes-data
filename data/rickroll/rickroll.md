## Overview

Rickrolling is a bait-and-switch prank that involves posting a hyperlink that is supposedly relevant to the topic at hand in an online discussion, but re-directs the viewer to the music video of "Never Gonna Give You Up," a 1987 dance pop single by English singer-songwriter Rick Astley.

## Origin

The meme originated from the imageboard 4chan as a spin-off of an earlier prank called duckrolling. The first recorded rickroll occurred in May 2007 on the /v/ (video games) board, where a link to Rick Astley's music video was disguised as a sneak preview for Grand Theft Auto IV.

## Spread

The practice spread rapidly across the internet, with notable instances including:
- The music video being played during Project Chanology protests against the Church of Scientology in February 2008.
- YouTube's front page on April 1, 2008, redirecting to the Rick Astley video.
- The New York Mets online poll in April 2008, where the song won with over five million votes.
- Rick Astley's appearance in the 2008 Macy's Thanksgiving Day Parade.
- The White House Twitter account posting a link to the video in July 2011.
- The Foo Fighters rickrolling audiences in 2015 and 2017.
- The Westworld Season 2 primer video in April 2018.

## Cultural Impact

Rickrolling has become a pervasive internet phenomenon, leading to resurgence in popularity of the song "Never Gonna Give You Up" and being used in various contexts, from political protests to corporate advertisements (e.g., CSAA Insurance Group's 2022 remake).
