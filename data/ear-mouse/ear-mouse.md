# Ear Mouse

## Overview
The Ear Mouse meme features a nude mouse with a piece of cow cartilage shaped like a human ear implanted on its back. The photograph, taken in 1997 by researchers Joseph and Charles Vacanti, was intended to demonstrate a technique for growing cartilage for reconstructive surgery.

## Origin
The image was originally used in scientific literature to showcase a biodegradable scaffold seeded with cow cartilage cells. Due to its striking appearance, it spread online as one of the earliest viral images.

## Spread and Controversy
The picture prompted a wave of protests against genetic engineering, despite the fact that no genetic engineering was involved—the ear was made from cow cartilage and could not be transplanted into a human due to immune rejection.

## Legacy
The Vacanti brothers continued their research, later using human cartilage cells to engineer tissues for patients, such as a 12-year-old boy with Poland's Syndrome. The ear mouse remains a notable example of how scientific imagery can be misinterpreted in the public sphere.
